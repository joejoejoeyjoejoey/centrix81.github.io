🔢 Algebra Fundamentals

What is Algebra?

Algebra uses letters (called variables) to represent unknown numbers. It helps us solve problems and find patterns!

Think of it this way:

If you have 3 boxes of cookies and each box has x cookies, you have 3x cookies total!

Solving Linear Equations

To solve an equation, get the variable alone on one side. Use inverse operations!

Example: Solve 2x + 5 = 13

2x + 5 = 13 Start with the equation
2x + 5 - 5 = 13 - 5 Subtract 5 from both sides
2x = 8 Simplify
2x ÷ 2 = 8 ÷ 2 Divide both sides by 2
x = 4 Solution! ✓

Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

When solving expressions with multiple operations, follow this order:

P Parentheses (3 + 2)
E Exponents 2³
M Multiplication 4 × 5
D Division 10 ÷ 2
A Addition 6 + 3
S Subtraction 9 - 4

Example: Solve 3 + 4 × 2²

= 3 + 4 × 4 (exponents first: 2² = 4)

= 3 + 16 (multiplication next: 4 × 4 = 16)

= 19 (addition last)

📊 Statistics & Probability

Measures of Central Tendency

These values describe the "center" of a data set:

Mean (Average)

Add all values, divide by the count

Mean = Sum of values ÷ Number of values

Median

The middle value when data is ordered

Data: 2, 5, 7, 8, 12 → Median = 7

Mode

The value that appears most often

Data: 3, 5, 5, 7, 5, 9 → Mode = 5

Probability

Probability measures how likely an event is to happen.

Probability = Favorable outcomes ÷ Total possible outcomes

🎲 Example: Rolling a Die

What's the probability of rolling a 3?

  • Favorable outcomes: 1 (just the number 3)
  • Total outcomes: 6 (numbers 1-6)
  • Probability = 1/6 ≈ 0.167 or about 16.7%
